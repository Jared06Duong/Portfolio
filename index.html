About Me

I’m a Computer Engineering (Co-op) student at the University of Guelph focused on embedded systems, software–hardware integration, and applied engineering design. I like working close to the system level, where code meets real constraints.

I’ve worked on automation and embedded systems in a manufacturing environment, improving monitoring logic and analyzing real-time sensor data. My projects span FPGA design, full-stack development, and autonomous hardware, including a VHDL-based ALU on FPGA, a Java/MySQL management system, and an Arduino-powered autonomous wheelchair prototype.

I’m comfortable across C/C++, Java, Python, VHDL, and modern dev tools. I like building practical systems that run outside the classroom.
